The Four Operations

Arithmetic is the oldest and most elementary branch of mathematics. It consists of the study of numbers and the properties of the traditional operations on them. Let's explore and practice the four basic operations below.

Addition (+)

Addition is the process of combining two or more numbers to get a sum. For example, 2 + 3 = 5.

+

Subtraction (-)

Subtraction is the process of taking away one number from another. For example, 5 - 2 = 3.

-

Multiplication (×)

Multiplication is repeated addition. For example, 3 × 4 means adding 3 four times: 3 + 3 + 3 + 3 = 12.

×

Division (÷)

Division is the process of splitting a number into equal parts. For example, 8 ÷ 2 = 4.

÷

BODMAS Rule

When solving an equation with multiple operations, it's crucial to follow the order of operations, commonly remembered as BODMAS:

Brackets → Orders (Powers) → Division → Multiplication → Addition → Subtraction

Let's look at a simple example. Suppose we have the expression: 4 + 3 × 2. According to BODMAS, we must perform the multiplication before the addition. So, 3 × 2 = 6, and then 4 + 6 = 10.
