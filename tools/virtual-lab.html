Fundamental Concepts

Ohm’s Law (V = I · R) states that the current flowing through a conductor is directly proportional to the potential difference (Voltage) and inversely proportional to the Resistance.

Power (P = V · I) is the rate at which electrical energy is converted into other forms. Increasing Voltage increases Power quadratically if Resistance is fixed.
